Discussion
  • What if People Create Bad Badges?
    • Moderate badges?
    • Let the bad badge sink to the bottom - natural selection? Yep
    • Create a system that only allows good badges
    • What makes a badge "bad"?
    • Poorly-defined badges (other other problems) will make the badge less desireable, will eventually fade away, I think
    • Spammy badges


  • Joe Schmoe creates a P2PU badge and gives it to all his friends (who are douchebags). They run around the web displaying their P2PU badges, which point to totally meaningless work. 
    • is there P2PU "branding" for badges? <- Good way to protect P2PU (no "P2PU badge" unless we are involved)
    • Probably too early to share this (if that's possible, not really) but a visual designer is working with me on badges for the Open Science course...with P2PU colors scheme worked in
      • link: https://docs.google.com/file/d/0B-243ski8vJiNzFrMWdlZUc0OWs/edit?usp=sharing (but my Hangout capability sucks right now, can chat more later)
    • We don't allow any user with the username "Joe Schmoe" to create badges <- That's a given
    • We require badge creators to get a tatoo of the badge, that way we know they create badges they care about
    • Devalue badges - Make it clear that a badge doesn't hold intrinsic value (value is associated with the work behind the badge and the community that reviewed it)
  • A badge is created for something totally rediculous - you are a cat poster (posted a cat picture on a forum)
  • A badge is created with no way to verify work?
    • What about the "evidence URL", linking back to the work. If it's not clickable, it'll be worth much less, so the "good" badges can be noticed.
  • A badge is created, with a rubric that is very easy to work through. Loads of people complete the required tasks, but there is a bottleneck in awarding the badge, which is a meaningless marker anyway. Now people are pissed off that they didn't get a pointless badge and are complining loudly and publicly
    • Should a centralized place for discussion around badges be created? or would that fragment things (the discussion about particular badges/courses) more?
  • Someone creates a general badge with a good idea behind it but then establishes a bad community around it.
    • like create a general 'open contributors source badge', but then get very picky about who gets the badge?
  • Someone creates 100s of badges.
  • Someone creates a cat badge and it goes viral
  • The "Official P2PU quality contributor" badges is created by someone we don't even know
    • this brings the idea of P2PU vetted or endorsed/approved badges...
