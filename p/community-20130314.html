Discussion

Engaging the Community at P2PU

  • Currently most community activity happens on the mailing list (and some in blog comments)
  • That's not entirely awesome:
    • Not enough "open" (not everyone knows about stuff that is going on, etc.)
    • Not enough "personality" (avatars, profiles, etc.)
    • Not enough "conversations" (new ideas, critique, etc.)
    • New people aren't finding their way into the community via the mailing list (because the mailing list doesn't let you see who we are)
    • Philipp's Question: Do we need to consider using Discourse - an open source discussion platform? 
    • Between Mechanical MOOC and Learning Creative Learning, over 30,000 new people have touched P2PU that haven't found their way to the mailing list.
    • What is the ask we should make to them to engage further?
      • IMHO At the moment, these are 2 totally seperate communities, who we treat and speak to very differently. 
      • I'd be interested to know how many of them even know that p2pu is running the MOOC they're signed up for.
      • It would be amazing to bring them in, but I think the ask is very different.
  • Would it make sense to move to discourse? http://www.discourse.org/ (it's open source and pretty awesome)
    • Host it ourselves? Yes, we can host it ourselves (there will also be a hosted solution in the future, which costs money, but maybe more cost-effective)
  • How to decide?
    • Change the way we use the mailing list instead of moving to a new tool?
    • Would this help us grow community? 
    • Surface cool stuff that is happening? 
    • Make P2PU more open/accessible? 
    • What are the risks? Not enough conversations? Forums will go stale?
