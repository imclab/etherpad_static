Badges (Rubric, ...)

  • Background: 
    • People don't know the difference between different badge systems - so Peter's aggregation is sorely needed
    • Work with OERs started with wikieducator - got to the point where assessment and accreditation became the biggest questions. 
    • Immersed in the badges community to learn more about how badges work, as a first step to devleoping the School of Badges
  • Idea: People should be able to create their own badge curriculum - hence the badge system design course  https://p2pu.org/en/groups/badge-system-design/
  • Peter's badge design rubric: (https://docs.google.com/spreadsheet/ccc?key=0Ak8Mcp1f93B1dHpOQVJnMzhJSm94Q1VsZHBySTZ3VlE#gid=0)
  • the idea of flipping assessment: http://criticaltechnology.blogspot.ca/2013/02/flipping-assessment.html
  • how I am implementing it within the challenge: http://criticaltechnology.blogspot.ca/2013/04/flipped-assessment-implemented.html

  • Questions:
    • Can you use this rubric to evaluate a particular suite of badges within a larger system?
      • Rubric is trying to more for guidance than evaluating an existing badge system (but it would apply). I do see the rubric working in most situations. it is what I am aspiring too.
    • P2PU is very uniquely user-generated content--(see Philipp's comment below) how does this less "formal" approach to badges fit into your rubric?
      • Thats the whole point IMO. helping people build there own accredition for their own curriculum, and courses they build for themselves or with a group. Heutagogy in practice, with accreditation, assessment is the next big challenge!
    • What is the role of experimentation in your badge system evaluation?

    • already these are great questions that could help make the rubric better... 
  • This is very focused on "accreditation" and using badges as an alternative to existing certification options - is there room for community based badges that have no intention to become accreditation? yes
    • Suggestion: expand the rubric, or make it explicit that the rubric focuses on a particular type of badges
  • Peter: All I know is I believe this community could provide valuable feedback of the rubric - think I need to find a more solid connectivity approach and join this call again. I really believe thier are some big experienced brains that could help here!!!! Let me experiment with some locations (I live on an island off the west coast of Canada, so G+ has sometimes been a problem, connectivity wise) Using a phone to call into the Hangout might be an idea, too.
    • +1 Would be great to be able to really dig into this, but we need the connection to work for that
