GET INVOLVED                                                              
FLOSS  Manuals is fantastic community to be a part of. There are manuals in  many languages on great desktop and android applications. The thing that  binds it all together is that these are resources written under a free  licence about applications also written under a Free Software licence. 

The  vast majority of all manuals on FM have been created and maintained by  people putting in voluntary contributions of their time. This creates a  very stable community and home for the manuals and allows us to do a lot  with a little.
                     

We are always looking for people who love writing about Free  Software. We maintain a database of writers, translators, facilitators  and workshop leaders. Please get in touch if you have work that needs  doing or if you are available for work. You can also do a lot of other  things to help: PR, write code, reach out to other projects, start a new  language community, raise money etc.
If you are interested in volunteering or want to find out more, the best thing is to join our mailing list and ask how you can get involved.
Writing, Editing and Fact Checking Manuals
Anybody  can contribute to a manual. To get access to the "write" area of the  site, all you need to do is to create a login ID on booki.flossmanuals.net.  By creating a login ID, you agree to release what you create here under  the GNU General Public License. Your login and profile information is  used to attribute copyright to you for what you write. We prefer that  you use your real name, but you can use a pseudonym if you don't mind  owning copyright under that pseudonym. Here are some of the activities  you get involved with:
  • writing new manuals
  • updating or improving existing manuals
  • proofreading manuals as they are being written
  • reviewing and fact checking.
Networking & Fundraising
There  are so many great manuals and resources as part of the FLOSS Manuals  network, in so many different languages. Sometimes it seems like we are  so busy writing them that we don’t have enough time to promote our  manuals as much as needed.We would really appreciate help spreading the  word.

Promotion can be a challenge for volunteer-based  communities. However our large user base works well to help us promote  the FLOSS Manuals. Here are some of the ways you can help:
  • write a blog post about FLOSS Manuals and our recent activites (see our news page)
  • embed our bookstore widget in your website
  • write a review of one of our manuals.
You  can help out by keeping us in the loop about projects and partners that  may be good for us to make contact with. If you see a funding  opportunities that could support our work, do let us know.
Helping with the Tech Infrastructure
Volunteering  for FM as a tech could be a great way to help many Free Software  communities in one go. It is also a great way to learn about innovative  publishing formats for online publishing, and online collaborative  software that can output to many different formats.

We are  looking for people with a good working knowledge of Python, Django, php  or css theming. FLOSS Manuals is build around the Booktype software. At the moment we have a few projects that we really need help with:
  • installing the latest version of Booktype and migrating our current content there.
  • working to set up a new Booktype install (and front page in php) for a new FLOSS Manuals in Spanish
  • theming existing sites with php and css.
Using FLOSS Manuals for Teaching
The  best road-testing of our documentation is when it is used in real-life  workshops. Please try out some of our manuals for teaching or as open  educational resources for online learning.
If you are looking to  prepare a workshop using Free Software, we would be delighted to work  with you to help you acheive this. We may be able to point you in the  direction of existing resources that fill your need. We may be able to  share techniques to help you overcome any barriers to teaching this  material.
Most of all we would welcome your feedback on using the  manuals, workbooks and courses at FLOSS Manuals so that together we can  keep them up-to-date and make them even better.
