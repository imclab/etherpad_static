Position: P2PU Learning Lead


About the Job

We are seeking a learning lead who wants radically improve the way people learn online. This is a full-time remote contract position that reports to the Executive Director.

The position has two goals:
  • Ensure that the P2PU learning model and platform foster engaging learning experiences for all users
  • Design and build assessment and certification mechanisms that scale and recognize higher order skills


Responsibilities

In order to achieve the goals, the learning lead will focus broadly on three areas:

Create awesome learning experiences
  • Design the core peer to peer learning model and assessment practices and translate them into features for the P2PU platform
  • Work closely with P2PU product manager and dev team to manage implementation of the learning and assessments features
  • Represent the "learning" focus and voice in all product design processes

Design assessment & certification mechanisms
  • Design an overall approach to assessment at P2PU that recognizes a broad range of skills including 21st century skills
  • Ensure that P2PU assessments are robust, valid, evidence-based and data-driven, and can scale
  • Design wireframes for new assessment features
  • Design alternative mechanisms to recognize users' achievements (incl. through badges) 
  • Support "schools" (e.g. School of Ed, Data or Games) to define community specific assessments and certification
  • Understanding of game mechanics, leveling up etc a bonus

Conduct and coordinate research
  • Design metrics and implement analytics to track, better understand, and improve all aspects of the learning experience (incl. retention, achievements, etc.)
  • Conduct original research in areas relevant to P2PU, including peer to peer learning online, peer assessment practices, and certification mechanisms for informal learners
  • Promote P2PU as a research platform and support a community of research partners


Profile / Requirements

  • Researcher - Solid understanding of learning and assessment theory and practice, but open to rethinking the conventional wisdom.
  • Builder - Able to translate evidence-based learning design into website features and UX.
  • Doer - able to manage own time and move multiple parallel projects forward, in a highly distributed environment.
  • Organizer - Strong organizational skills, attention to detail and reliability. You don't let things slip through the cracks.
  • Sharer - Comfortable working "in the open", sharing work early and often and engaging the community.
  • Occasional Traveler - Able to travel occasionally for conferences, meet-ups and presentations.


Bonus Points

  • Previous contributions to P2PU.
  • Contributions to other open projects and communities.
  • An eye and passion for design (typography, branding, etc).
  • Experience working as part of an asynchronous distributed team.
