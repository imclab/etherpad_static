Research:

  • Idea was to make P2PU a Research Hub
    • Recruit and support researchers
    • Share data with them
  • Realized that this is a big job and takes a lot of work
  • At the same time we've loved working with June (U o Maryland)
  • So instead of building up this community of lots of people, we thought it might be more manageable to build relationships with 2-4 people like June
    • Research is designed collaboratively
    • Results help us AND are interesting research in the field
  • Question -> To recruit awesome people, what should we do? How should we start it?
    • Talk/blog about possibility to collaborate on research
    • Reach out to Steven Downes and ask him for recommendations? [Billy will bring it up with him when they speak]
    • More cat pictures (where do we need to put them?)
    • Research with universities are slow to plan and fund
      • Working with students is easier
      • There are pros and cons of working with students
    • Characteristics of research relationship we like:
      • Design questions together  - this will be something that might be tricky - not all academics even know what their questions are. That's true, but those types of collaborations will take more time and have higher risk of not producing anything. Working with people who have questions makes getting started easier. 
      • Overlap between researchers' questions AND P2PU trying to figure out how to do what we do better
      • Needs to be easy, not require major funding to get started
      • No huge delays in getting started (moving from idea, to doing)
